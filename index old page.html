Mario Guzmán — Experimental writer, media artist & creative robotics designer

Mario Guzmán is a Mexican writer and media artist with a focus on the links between culture, language, art, and education. Mario also explores the communication between human and non-human and the process of encoding perception and storytelling through technology. Outcomes include experimental cinema, non-linearity, mapping, generative literature, robotics and interactive media.

Quetzalcoatl-bot. Between code and codex

Interactive robotic installation
Robot: 72 cm x 22 cm x 16 cm
Projection: 1.90 m x 1.90 m

Quetzalcoatl-bot is a representation of the Mesoamerican deity of light, knowledge, and art. Through robotics, mapping and computer vision, this mechanic feathered serpent becomes a contemporary storyteller of ancient myths. It's also a counter-writing platform to experiment with the coexistence of codex and creative coding.

Quetzalcoatl-bot. Between code and codex (2015)


Video installation + Robotics
Robotics, computer vision, code, real-time image capture, interaction, projection mapping, generative writing, acrylic, paper, Arduino, Pir, Infrared light. Robot 72 cm x 22 cm x 16 cm Projection 1.90m x 1.90m.


Quetzalcoatl-bot, entre códigos y códices, de la serie Arqueología futurista, es una instalación que vincula -a partir de la implementación de un sistema de captura de movimiento y robótica- dos formas de interpretar el vínculo entre palabra e imagen: el código de programación y la información cultural contenida en los códices prehispánicos. La instalación consta de una proyección cenital dentro de cuyos márgenes circula Quetzalcóatl-bot. Sus movimientos, activados por el movimiento del público, son seguidos por una cámara infrarroja que activa una animación generativa de vida artificial. Los trazos generados develan/proyectan una serie de códices subyacentes vinculados a la organización del universo y al origen del lenguaje.

Generative Oracle. from pulse to letter

Digital literature interactive installation
Dimensions: 100 x 90 x 50 cm

The Generative Oracle. from pulse to letter is a technical-symbolic interface that operates as an abstraction of oracular practices to produce enigmatic texts destined to behave as contemporary omens. The work is part of an experimental writing project that focuses on the cooperation between human and machine.

Generative Oracle. from pulse to letter (2014)


Digital literature interactive installation
Microcontroller, generative software, acrylic, infrared sensors. 100 x 90 x 50 cm


The Generative Oracle. from pulse to letter is a technical-symbolic interface that operates as an abstraction of oracular practices to produce enigmatic texts destined to behave as contemporary omens. The work is part of an experimental writing project that focuses on the cooperation between human and machine.

Machine de transcodage bit-cellule

Mapping

The Machine de transcodage bit-cellule, is an audioVisual exploration of computation substrate and its evolution into ever-digitally-mediated organic cells. From binary light and logic gates that undergo the complexity of discrete agent simulation, to ASCII-defined cellular forms. This piece placed at the House of Henri II as the inauguration mapping of the Electronic Art Festival ZERO1 at La Rochelle, France.
